Is online gambling legal in Canada?

Online gambling in Canada operates within a complex legal framework. While gambling falls under provincial jurisdiction, the rise of the internet has blurred traditional boundaries. The Atlantic Lottery Corporation (ALC) permits online gambling, allowing Canadians to participate in offshore online casinos and sports. However, it’s generally illegal for casinos and sportsbooks to operate online within Canada. Despite this, Canadians can still access and play on gambling websites based outside the country. In 2004, the British Columbia Lottery Corporation took a significant step by launching Canada’s first legal online casino, PlayNow.com.

Sports betting has been legal in Canada since 1985, and the recent introduction of single-game betting is expected to enhance the sports wagering experience. While the country doesn’t grant licenses for online gambling, many Canadians turn to offshore sites for their gambling needs. It’s essential to note that while some forms of online gambling are permitted, others, especially those operating within Canada’s borders, are considered illegal. As always, individuals should consult local laws and regulations before engaging in online gambling activities.
